# Day 6 — Go-to-Market + Distribution

**Duration:** 60 min · **Theme:** Building the thing is half the job. The other half is getting someone to use it.

---

## Today's goal

Write a 1-sentence pitch for your project. Polish your GitHub README. Ship one real action to get a real person to try your tool.

---

## The one idea you need

Most people who build things stop at "I built it." The builders who actually matter ask: **"Who's going to use this, and how do I reach them?"**

Getting your first user is a skill. It's not marketing magic — it's just three moves: personal network, social sharing, and search. Today you pick one and actually do it.

---

## The 5-second pitch

Every project needs a one-sentence pitch that any human can understand in 5 seconds.

Format:
> "I built **[name]**. It **[does one thing]** for **[specific who]**. Try it: **[URL]**."

**Good examples:**
- "I built Study Buddy. It turns any textbook paragraph into a 5-question quiz. Try it: study-buddy.replit.app"
- "I built Morning Brief. It texts me a weather update + my top 3 tasks every morning at 7am."
- "I built Rate My Outfit. DM it a photo and it rates your fit with specific suggestions."

**Bad example:**
- "It uses AI to help people do things better." ← Too vague to mean anything.

If you can't write the pitch, your project isn't specific enough yet. Fix the project, then write the pitch.

---

## Build steps

**Step 1 — Write your pitch (10 min)**

Write your 1-sentence pitch using the format above. Post it in Discord.

Instructor gives live feedback. If it's vague, you rewrite it. Keep going until it passes the "would I actually click that link?" test.

**Step 2 — Polish your GitHub README (20 min)**

A README is marketing, not documentation. It should answer in under 10 seconds: What does this do? Why does it exist? How do I try it?

Tell Replit Agent:

> "Update the README.md in my project to match this template. Fill in all the details for my project:
>
> # [Project name]
> [1-sentence description]
>
> ## What it does
> [2–3 sentences]
>
> ## Try it
> [live URL]
>
> ## Built with
> - Python
> - Replit
> - AI (via Replit Managed AI Integrations)"

Push to GitHub: commit message `"Day 6: polish README + GTM prep"`.

**Step 3 — Add a share button (10 min)**

Tell Agent: *"Add a 'Share this tool' button to my app that copies the live URL to the clipboard when clicked."*

This is a tiny feature with real value — it makes it easy for users to spread your tool.

**Step 4 — Ship one real GTM move (during class)**

Pick exactly one and do it now, during the session:

| Move | Who can do it |
|---|---|
| Post in `#day-6-ships` with your live URL + 1-sentence pitch | Everyone |
| Text a parent, sibling, or friend the link and ask them to try it | Everyone |
| Send to a parent-approved friend outside camp | Everyone |
| Post to a relevant subreddit or TikTok | **Only with explicit parent approval** |
| Record a 30-second screen recording and share it | Everyone |

Do the move. Don't describe the move you'll do later. Do it now.

> ✅ Ship proof: post evidence of your GTM move in `#day-6-ships`. "I texted my cousin" + screenshot counts.
